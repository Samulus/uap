What is uap?

uap is server software that allows you to stream your music collection to any computer with a modern web browser and an internet connection.

Why are we creating it?

The team decided that this was a problem that many people may have and after studying the existing programs out there we came to the conclusion that there wasn't a good alternative available.

Problems with existing software

  • Ampache (http://ampache.org/)
    • Excellent software but not portable enough, requires entire LAMP stack to get running
  • Spotify (https://www.spotify.com/us/)
    • Proprietary: could disappear at any moment
    • Advertisements in free plan
    • DRM-laden
  • Subsonic (http://www.subsonic.org/)
    • Good but monolithic: tries to do everything, not just music
    • No longer open source
  • Grooveshark (...)
      • DMCA'd to death =\

Deliverables

  1. Functional backend (SQLite3 / taglib / bottle.py completely integrated)
  2. Barebones client (enough to view a list of songs and play each)
  3. Client with view by Artist support
  4. Client with view by Album support
  5. Client with view by Genre support
  6. Client with view by Year support
  7. Client with search support
  8. Jukebox support in the backend
  9. Jukebox support in the client
  10. WebGL music visualizations and other fun surprises

Use Case Analysis

The primary use case for our program are people who wish to stream their music to their various devices while they're away from home without having to juggle multiple copies of their entire music library across devices.

If time permits we also wish to include a "jukebox" mode where instead of music being streamed to a specific client, the client(s) get to play music on the server. This would be useful at a party and would allow multiple people to vote for music to enqueue etc.

Database Design

We intend to use SQLite3 with a few tables for Artists / Albums / Songs to maximize performance and portability.

Time Dependent Features

At a bare minimum we intend to support streaming music to multiple clients who are connected at once.

The list of features that will be added / removed depending on time is exactly the same: If we have enough time we will add support for jukebox mode and potentially music visualizations. Otherwise these features will be omitted from the final project.
